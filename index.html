Guidelines for
Pediatric Amblyopia

A practical reference for clinical annotators marking entities, relationships, and attributes in ophthalmology notes.

§ 1
How Annotation Works
Each annotated note is built from three layers: spans, attributes, and relationships.
1
Mark Spans
Highlight minimal continuous text expressing a concept. Assign one label per span.
→
2
Set Attributes
Add assertion / experiencer / temporality only when the text explicitly indicates a non-default value.
→
3
Add Relationships
Link spans that the text connects: laterality to diagnosis, adherence to treatment, etc.
→
4
QC Check
Verify every laterality/severity/pattern in eligible text is linked. No structured-data inferences.
Golden rule: Annotate what is written, not what you think the clinician meant. Do not infer missing laterality, missing time, or missing diagnoses from context or structured fields.
§ 2
Should I Annotate This?
Walk through this decision flow when you're unsure whether a piece of text is eligible.
Is this free-text narrative — not a table, grid, or templated pull?
Structured data blocks (VA tables, refraction grids, slit lamp template rows, aligned columns) are never eligible, even if they appear as text in the note.
Is it in HPI, Assessment, Plan, or other clearly clinician-authored free text?
Eligible sections: HPI / Interval History, Assessment, Plan / MDM, and other narrative lines clearly written by the clinician. Physical exam template blocks remain ineligible even if they look like text.
Is this a ROS checklist — including "denies…" symptom lines?
ROS checklists are always ineligible, even when symptoms are named. "Denies blurred vision" in a checklist should not be annotated.
✓
Annotate this text
This text is eligible. Annotate all relevant concept spans, set attributes where indicated, and add relationships. Use minimal spans and only annotate what is explicitly written.
✗
Do not annotate — structured data
Skip tables, grids, and structured blocks. Exception: if a clearly narrative interpretation sentence is embedded inside a structured block, annotate only that sentence.
✗
Do not annotate — templated section
Physical exam / base exam sections with templated pulls are ineligible. Skip the entire block unless a narrative interpretation line is clearly embedded.
✗
Do not annotate — ROS checklist
ROS checklists are always ineligible, including "denies…" lines. If the same symptom appears in the HPI as narrative, annotate it there.
§ 3
Eligible vs. Ineligible Text
Annotate for high recall within eligible free-text. Avoid noise from structured pulls and boilerplate.
✓ Annotate (Eligible)
  • HPI / Interval History
  • Assessment section
  • Plan / Medical Decision Making
  • Narrative lines embedded in structured sections
  • Family history (set experiencer=family)
  • Past ocular history when in free-text
✗ Do Not Annotate (Ineligible)
  • Physical exam / Base exam structured blocks
  • VA tables, refraction grids, slit lamp rows
  • ROS checklists ("denies blurred vision")
  • Boilerplate counseling without patient-specific detail
  • Administrative boilerplate
  • Copy-forwarded duplicate sections (annotate only most current)
Span selection: Always use minimal spans — the smallest continuous stretch of text that expresses the concept. Do not include adjacent words that belong to other concepts (laterality, severity, etc.). One concept per span. No discontinuous annotations.
§ 4
Attributes
Attributes describe a single span. Change them only when the note text explicitly indicates a non-default value.

Note: In pediatric notes, parents often report symptoms on behalf of the child. This does not change experiencer — it remains "patient."
Temporality
Is the concept ongoing or historical?
current default
Ongoing at time of note, even if it started earlier.
historical

"Esotropia s/p BMR surgery." → procedure is historical
Note: Something that happened in the past but is still active = current. Historical + present assertion is valid: "past procedure, still relevant."

Scoping Rules

Laterality Scoping
  • Link laterality to every concept it explicitly modifies in the local phrase
  • If stated once in a bullet/problem-line, link to each item in that coordinated line
  • OK to repeat across a coordinated phrase: "both eyes — myopia and astigmatism"
Don't Do This
  • Don't carry laterality across unrelated sentences unless explicitly repeated
  • Don't infer laterality from VA table values in structured blocks
  • Don't automatically carry "both eyes" forward to a concept in the next sentence
§ 6
Common Mistakes
The most frequent annotation errors — and what to do instead.
Laterality
✓ Do
Link laterality only when explicitly stated in the free text.
"Amblyopia left eye" → Amblyopia has_laterality Left eye
✗ Don't
Do not infer laterality from structured VA tables or exam blocks.
Seeing "OS 20/80" in a VA table does NOT mean you can link Left to a diagnosis in the narrative.
ROS vs. HPI
✓ Do
Annotate symptoms mentioned in narrative HPI or Assessment.
"Mom reports the left eye crosses intermittently" → Crossing + Laterality + Pattern
✗ Don't
Do not annotate symptoms from ROS checklists, even named ones.
"Denies blurred vision" in a ROS checklist → skip entirely
Compound "-ic" Diagnoses
✓ Do
Split compound phrases into separate concepts when no single ontology match exists.
"anisometropic esotropia" → Anisometropia + Esotropia (two separate spans)
✗ Don't
Do not invent subtype semantics for compound phrases not in the ontology.
Do not create a fake "DiagAnisometropicEsotropia" concept or treat one as a modifier of the other.
Copy-Forward Text
✓ Do
When text is duplicated in the note, annotate only the most current version.
Prefer last Assessment/Plan over "Previous Exam Notes" or earlier occurrences.
✗ Don't
Do not annotate older copy-forward duplicates unless they contain unique clinical info not present elsewhere.
If the same HPI block appears twice, skip the older one.

Skip diagnoses in structured pulls/tables.

has_lateralityhas_onsethas_patternhas_severity

Exam Finding Exam
Functional/observed findings in narrative Assessment/Plan — not structured exam blocks.
Skip the physical exam block by default; only annotate narrative interpretation lines.
has_lateralityhas_patternhas_severity

Test Test
Test being performed, ordered, or attempted — in Plan/narrative.
Skip tests listed only in structured exam tables.
has_fixation_context
"attempt Teller visual acuity"→Visual acuity test
"stereo testing at near next visit"→Stereoacuity test + Near
Clinical Measure CM
The measure/construct itself (not the act of testing). E.g. "evaluate visual acuity."
Skip numeric values from structured tables.
has_fixation_context
"re-check near visual acuity"→Visual Acuity + Near
"distance stereoacuity remains poor"→Stereoacuity + Distance
Treatment Tx
Treatment in use, recommended, adjusted, refused, or not started.
Skip treatments only in structured prescription blocks unless narrative.
has_adherencehas_regimenhas_statushas_phasehas_outcome
"patch 2 hours/day"→Patching + Frequency
"never really wore glasses"→Glasses + Poor adherence
Procedure Proc
Surgery or procedures in eligible free-text (HPI, POH, Assessment, Plan).
Often historical — use temporality=historical for s/p procedures.
temporality=historical
"s/p BMR"→ProcStrabismusSurgery (historical)
"cataract extraction"→ProcCataractExtraction
Outcome Out
Discrete outcome label explicitly stated as result of treatment or condition course.
Don't use for course words like "improving" — those are Pattern.
Treatment has_outcome →
"reverse amblyopia"→Reverse amblyopia
"patch allergy"→Bandage allergy
Adherence Ad
Adherence quality or barriers for a treatment — annotate what is written.
Don't infer adherence. Requires explicit text evidence.
Treatment has_adherence →
"never wore them"→Poor adherence → Glasses
"refuses patch"→Patch refusal → Patching
"lost glasses"→Lost glasses
Plan Plan
Plan actions in free-text: follow-up, referral, monitoring, adjustment.
Skip admin boilerplate without patient-specific info.
has_scheduling
"RTC 3 months"→Follow-up + Duration
"referral to strabismus clinic"→Referral
Social Determinants SDoH
Social/structural factors explicitly affecting care access or adherence.
Don't infer — only annotate when explicitly stated in the text.
co-occurs with Adherence
"missed visits due to transportation"→Transportation
"cannot afford glasses"→Ability to afford healthcare
"needs interpreter"→Speaking language
§ 8
Modifier Concepts
Context and modifier spans linked to primary concepts via relationships.
Laterality
Lat
Explicitly stated eye references: left/right, OD/OS/OU, both eyes, fellow eye, amblyopic eye.
"left" → Left eye (OS)
"both eyes" → Both eyes (OU)
"amblyopic eye" → Amblyopic Eye
Severity
Sev
Severity words when explicitly stated: mild, moderate, severe.
"severe amblyopia" → Severe → has_severity
"mild esotropia" → Mild → has_severity
Pattern
Pat
Course/behavior over time: intermittent, constant, improving, worsening, stable, residual, recurrent.
"amblyopia improving" → Improving
"residual esotropia" → Residual
"intermittent exotropia" → Intermittent
Fixation Context
Fix
Near / Distance when explicitly stated with a test or clinical measure. Do not infer.
"near visual acuity" → Near → Visual Acuity
"cover test at distance" → Distance → Cover test
Temporal
Temp
Date/time, age of onset, frequency, or duration. Tag spans only — no normalization. Age is for onset only, not demographics.
"since age 2" → Age → has_onset
"2 hours/day" → Frequency → has_regimen
"for 3 months" → Duration → has_regimen
Status
Stat
Explicit status cues for treatments: new, continued, unchanged, stopped, restarted, deferred.
"continue glasses" → Continued
"will not patch" → Deferred
"stop atropine" → Stopped
Phase
Phas
Phase of treatment explicitly stated: tapering, trial.
"trial of atropine" → Trial → has_phase
"taper patching" → Tapering → has_phase
Dose
Dose
Dosage expressions when explicitly stated. Tag the span; link to treatment when possible.
"atropine 1 drop nightly" → Dosage
"0.01% atropine" → Dosage
